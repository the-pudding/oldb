The opening line of Laicus; Or, the Experiences of a Layman in a Country Parish. by Lyman Abbott

ABOUT sixty miles north of New York city,—not as the crow flies, for of the course of that bird I have no knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief, but as the Mary Powell ploughs her way up the tortuous channel of the Hudson river,—lies the little village of Wheathedge.
