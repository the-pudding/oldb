The Making of William Edwards; or, The Story of the Bridge of Beauty

G. Linnaeus (George Linnaeus) Banks

It was a sad day for Mrs. Edwards, of Eglwysilan, when her well-loved husband, on his return from Llantrissant market one sultry Friday in the autumn of 1721, in attempting to cross the River Taff, failed to observe its rising waters, missed the ford, and was carried down the stream, a drowning man.
