Robert Orange Being a Continuation of the History of Robert Orange

John Oliver Hobbes

One afternoon during the first weeks of October, 1869, while wind, dust, and rain were struggling each for supremacy in the streets, a small yellow brougham, swung in the old-fashioned style on cumbersome springs and attached to a pair of fine greys, was standing before the Earl of Garrow’s town residence in St. James’s Square.
