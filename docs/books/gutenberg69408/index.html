The shoemaker A powerful picture of nature, adapted from Hal Reid's famous drama of the same name

Olive Harper

In one of the small side streets that end in the Bowery, on the East Side, is a row of small and dilapidated buildings, which once, in the early days of New York, were the dwellings of fashionable people, but which are now occupied by poor but industrious people.
